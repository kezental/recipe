Shirly Temple

A Shirley Temple is a non-alcoholic mixed drink traditionally made with ginger ale, a splash of grenadine, and is garnished with a maraschino cherry. Modern Shirley Temple recipes may use lemon-lime soda or lemonade, and sometimes orange juice, in part or in whole, include a citrus wedge or slice as a garnish, and use maraschino cherry juice. The drink may have been invented at Chasen's, a restaurant in West Hollywood, California, to serve then-child actress Shirley Temple. However, other claims to its origin have been made.

    Ingredients
  • Ice
  • 1 cup ginger ale or lemon-lime soda (8 ounces; 235ml)
  • Maraschino Cherries
  • 2 tablespoons homemade or store-bought grenadine (1 ounce; 30ml)
  • (Optional) Lime or Lemon wedge
    Instructions
  1. Fill glass with ice
  2. Pour in the Lemon Lime Soda or Ginger Ale
  3. Using a spoon, gently stir grenadine into ginger ale until combined
  4. Add as many maraschino cherries as you want
  5. Mix
  6. (Optional) Add lemon/lime wedge
